Wedding / Afternoon

St Andrew's Presbyterian Church

June 21, 1:00 PM

16 Merivale Lane, Merivale

We will be having our wedding ceremony and afternoon reception at the wonderful St Andrew's Presbyterian Church at Rangi Ruru Girl's School in Merivale, not only because it is still standing, but because it is a beautiful church building and we both wanted it as our first choice! We could hardly pass up that consensus, and so we locked it in nice and early.

It was originally built in 1857 opposite the central Christchurch Hospital, but with nearby commercial development in the 20th Century, numbers dwindled and the church was in danger of having to shut its doors, so it was cut into sections and moved to the school where it reopened in 1987 to function as both a local parish and the school chapel. During the move it was also renovated and so it is still in wonderful condition!

Our wedding starts at 1:00 PM, and the afternoon reception will follow immediately afterward in the Helen Kitson Function Centre at the school. We will walk over from the church after the wedding, as the centre on the same campus, and we will have time for afternoon tea and speeches after skits and embarrassing stories! We hope to see you there before we leave for photos around 3:00 PM, but feel free to stay and socialise after that!

Evening Reception

Newbery Lodge

June 21, 6:00 PM

15 Newbery Street, Opawa

After all the photos, we will be having an evening reception at the secluded Newbury Lodge Function Centre out in Opawa at the foot of the Port Hills, surrounded by green ivy and gardens away from the bustle of the city. We considered many different venues before settling on this one, but after we drove out and had a look we were convinced!

It was originally a homestead built by the Newbery back in the mid 19th Century, and was one of the many grand private residences in Christchurch over that period. Over the years, it was expanded and renovated to become the expansive two story lodge that it is now, and it has been used since 1970 for events and functions while still being privately owned. It is surrounded by beautiful gardens that are well kept all year round.

Since we both have large families, we are having a smaller evening reception as well for our family and relatives who have travelled from out of town and who probably won't see us again before we leave for the US. Aim to be there around 6:00 PM so that you can have a look around before being seated for dinner at 6:30 PM. We have the lodge until 12:00 AM, after which the night is old and the morning service is beckoning!

Put together by the groom, with the help from his beautiful bride!
